The Information Commissioner encourages political parties to engage with the electorate using leaflets, addressed letters, emails, etc.

"It is vital in any democratic society that political parties and campaigners are able to communicate effectively with voters. But it is equally vital for the integrity of elections and democracy that all organisations involved in political campaigning handle and process personal data in a way that is compliant with data protection law. In recent years political campaigning has become increasingly sophisticated as new digital technologies and communication tools developed rapidly. Campaigners now use ever more innovative techniques to attempt to understand their potential voters and target them with political messaging."

Why the Gloucestershire Independents are permitted to use your data in this way

As a registered national political party in the UK, the Gloucestershire Independents are entitled to a copy of the full Electoral Register under the Representation of the People Regulations 2001. The full Electoral Register is different to the open Electoral Register as it contains details of all voters in the UK, including those who have opted out of the open register. We do contact voters outside of election periods, however, we will honour opt outs for this type of communication upon request. At a Parliamentary election, we are also permitted to send one Election Communication via Royal Mail to all electors under Section 91 of the Representation of the People Act 1983.

Please be aware that in a democratic society like the United Kingdom, you cannot opt out of unaddressed political leaflets but we will do our best to respect your wishes if you do not wish to receive communication of this nature from the Party.

Email Communications

If you have provided us with your email address and have given us permission to do so, we may send you information about Gloucestershire Independents and our local campaigns via email. You can opt-out of these emails at any time using one of the following methods:

  • Click the unsubscribe link provided at the bottom of our email messages.
  • Complete the "Opt Out" form on our website (above).
  • Please allow up to 28 days for your email opt-out requests to take effect in our systems.

SMS Messages

If you've provided your mobile phone number and have given us permission, we may send you text messages. To stop receiving SMS messages, you can follow the opt-out instructions in the text message or complete our "Opt Out" form. Please allow 28 days for your request to become effective in our systems.

Post and Telephone

We may contact you by post or telephone for campaigning, informing you about our values and policies, and verifying your eligibility to donate to support our cause. If you are registered with the Telephone Preference Service (TPS), we will respect your preferences unless you have opted in to receive communications from us. If you are not on the TPS register, you can request to stop receiving calls by informing our operators during our calls to you.

Online Advertising

We will never use your data to advertise online or through social media.
