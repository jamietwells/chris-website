Our Cotswolds

On 2nd May 2023, voters across the Cotswolds will have the opportunity to vote in a referendum to keep or to replace the mayoral system with a modern committee system.

Our Cotswolds is an independent group campaigning to raise awareness of the benefits of a collaborative committee system.

If you love our District #VoteCommittee

The Referendum question on the ballot paper will be:

How would you like Cotswold District Council to be run? By a Leader who is elected by the councillors. This is how the Council is run now. By one or more Committees made up of elected Councillors. This would be a change from how the Council is run now. X

To improve democracy in the Cotswolds, #VoteCommittee

Read more

In the Cotswold District, councillors elect a 'Strong Leader' to rule over us for for years. He and his hand-picked Cabinet (seven councillors) make all the decisions.

They have absolute power, very little scrutiny and almost no accountability until four years later when we vote again.

The public, and the rest of our elected councillors, are mostly locked out of the decision-making process.

We think that means our community voices are being ignored.
